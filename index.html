Why this exists
"There is a particular kind of loneliness in not being able to read the way the world expects you to.

Not because the words are hard. Because the page itself feels hostile."

I built ReadEasy on a Saturday night when my university's reading software crashed and support wasn't available. I needed to study. So I built what I needed.

I am a philosophy student. I believe that once ideas are accessible, people are accessible to ideas. That is the only thing ReadEasy is trying to do — remove the distance between a person and the words in front of them.

We are not here to tell you something is wrong with you. We are not here to diagnose, categorise, or target anyone. We are just here. If you are finding it difficult to read — for any reason, in any language, at any hour — this is your access.

No account. No cost. No explanation required.

What it does

Tools that work with your mind

Not designed for a diagnosis. Designed for a person.

🧠
Bionic Reading
Bolds the first half of every word so your eyes anchor faster and your brain fills the rest automatically.
📏
Reading Ruler
A highlight band follows your cursor so you never lose your place on the page.
🔊
Read Aloud
Highlights each word as it's spoken. Adjustable speed. Click any single word to hear it instantly.
⚡
Speed Reader
One word at a time, at your pace. Uses the Optimal Recognition Point so your brain processes faster.
📚
Tap to Define
Click any word for an instant definition, phonetics, and example sentence. No switching tabs.
🔦
Focus Mode
Dims everything except the paragraph you're reading. For when the whole page feels overwhelming.
📄
PDF Upload
Drag and drop any PDF and read it with all accessibility features active.
🎨
Colour Themes
7 background colours including cream, blue, yellow, and dark — because white isn't always best.
🔤
Dyslexia Fonts
Lexend, Atkinson Hyperlegible, OpenDyslexic, Georgia — fonts designed for easier reading.
Our only belief

Access is not a feature.
It is a right.

ReadEasy does not ask you to prove that you need it. It does not ask you to fit a category, hold a diagnosis, or explain yourself to anyone. The difficulty you feel when you read is real. You are not broken. The tools were just not built for you. Until now.
